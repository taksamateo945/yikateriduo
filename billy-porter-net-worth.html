Billy Porter Net Worth: How Much Does the Pose Star Make?

Billy Porter is a multi-talented actor, singer, director, producer, and writer who has won acclaim and awards for his work on Broadway and television.

He is best known for his role as Pray Tell on the FX series Pose, which earned him an Emmy in 2019.

He has also starred in musicals such as Kinky Boots, Grease, and Smokey Joe’s Cafe, and released several albums of soulful songs.

But how much money does Billy Porter make, and what is his net worth?

How much money does Billy Porter make?

Billy Porter has a net worth of an estimated $1 million as of August 2023.

Porter has earned his money through his success as an actor, director, producer, vocal coach, and performer.

Some of his notable projects include:

  • Kinky Boots: Porter originated the role of Lola, a drag queen who helps a struggling shoe factory owner, in this Broadway musical based on a 2005 film. He won a Tony Award, a Drama Desk Award, and an Outer Critics Circle Award for his performance. He also reprised the role in London’s West End and on tour.
  • Pose: Porter plays Pray Tell, the MC and mentor of the ballroom scene in New York City in the late 1980s and early 1990s, in this FX series created by Ryan Murphy. He won an Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Drama Series in 2019, making history as the first openly gay Black man to do so. He also received two Golden Globe nominations and a Critics’ Choice Award for his role.
  • Cinderella: Porter portrays the Fairy Godmother in this 2021 musical film adaptation of the classic fairy tale, starring Camila Cabello as Cinderella. He brings his signature flair and style to the role, wearing a stunning gold gown and delivering empowering messages to the heroine.
  • Billy Porter Presents the Soul of Richard Rodgers: This is Porter’s fourth studio album, released in 2017. It features reinterpretations of songs by composer Richard Rodgers, with guest appearances by artists such as Cynthia Erivo, Leslie Odom Jr., Pentatonix, and India.Arie.
